The Economics of Automation

Automation ROI is not about replacing people. It is about compressing cycle time, reducing error, and increasing throughput.

Real-World Example: Bank Loan Processing

Baseline (Manual)
  • Cycle time: 14 days
  • Cost per decision: $45
  • Default rate: 4.2%
  • Annual Cost (10k loans): $450,000
With Ferrum (Year 1)
  • Cycle time: 4 hours
  • Cost per decision: $8
  • Default rate: 2.1%
  • Annual Cost: $80,000
Net Year 1 ROI: 258%

How to Calculate Your ROI

Automate tasks, not roles. Look for reviewing documents, applying rules, validating compliance, or generating reports. Must have clear inputs and outputs.

Capture labor cost per execution, volume, error/rework rate, and cycle-time cost (lost revenue due to delay).

Conservative estimates: 70-90% reduction in time per task. Near-zero error rates. 10-100x throughput increase.

Labor efficiency + Error elimination + Throughput value + Risk reduction.

Typical ROI by Type:
• Document workflows: 300-800%
• Revenue decisioning: >500%
• Compliance: Justified by risk alone
